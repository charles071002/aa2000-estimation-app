# Fire Detection and Alarm System (FDAS) — Market Research: Mean Average Pricing (Philippines)

This document presents mean average prices for FDAS components and labor in the Philippines, based on publicly listed prices, tender data, and supplier quotes (NCR and major regions). Values are suitable for cost computation in project estimates and BOQs. Prices are in Philippine Peso (₱), exclusive of VAT unless noted.

---

## 1. Detectors

- **The mean average price of a conventional smoke detector (photoelectric) is 800 pesos.**  
  (Reference range: budget standalone ₱439–₱549; wired conventional ₱924–₱1,100; premium up to ₱1,500.)

- **The mean average price of an addressable smoke detector is 2,200 pesos.**  
  (Reference: addressable points typically 1.5×–2.5× conventional; Asenware AW-D101–type and similar.)

- **The mean average price of a conventional heat detector (fixed temperature / rate-of-rise) is 1,200 pesos.**  
  (Reference: Eaton-type ~₱1,475; Dahua addressable heat ~₱775–₱1,598; conventional mid-range ~₱1,000–₱1,500.)

- **The mean average price of an addressable heat detector is 1,500 pesos.**  
  (Reference: aligned with addressable heat products in local listings.)

- **The mean average price of a multi-sensor detector (e.g. smoke + heat) is 3,000 pesos.**  
  (Reference: combination/intelligent detectors typically ₱2,500–₱4,000.)

- **The mean average price of a flame detector (UV/IR, industrial) is 15,000 pesos.**  
  (Reference: industrial flame detection ₱8,000–₱25,000+; mean for standard specs ~₱15,000.)

- **The mean average price of a gas detector (fire alarm–related) is 5,500 pesos.**  
  (Reference: specialty gas detection modules ~₱3,000–₱8,000.)

- **The mean average price of an “Other” or unspecified detector type is 1,800 pesos.**  
  (Reference: fallback between conventional smoke and multi-sensor.)

## 8. Notes for Cost Computation

- **Detector mix:** Use detector type from survey (Smoke, Heat, Flame, Gas, Multi-sensor, Other) and system type (Conventional vs Addressable) to select the appropriate detector and FACP unit price.
- **Notification devices:** Use notification appliance type (Bell, Horn, Strobe, Horn-Strobe) and MCP count from survey; apply respective mean prices.
- **Cabling:** Use cable length from survey × cable price per meter; choose FPLR/FPL or FPLP based on cable type if specified.
- **Labor:** Multiply total man-days (from manpower breakdown) by daily rate; add 12% for testing and commissioning and ₱800 for documentation where applicable.
- **VAT:** Apply 12% VAT to subtotal for final billable amount when required.

*Sources: Philippine supplier listings (CCTVPinoy, Maximum Philippines, Guard-All, RS Online PH, VMIDirect, Galleon, Philcon Prices), tender data (e.g. DBP FDAS), and industry benchmarks. Prices as of 2024–2025; revise periodically.*
